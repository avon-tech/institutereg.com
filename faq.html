Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What types of stem cells do you use?
We use only VSELs, which stands for "very small embryonic-like" stem cells.

Where do these stem cells come from?
VSELs come from the patient's peripheral blood.

Are these stem cells autologous (derived from the patient) or allogeneic (derived from healthy donors)?
VSELs are autologous (derived from the patient).

How long is the treatment?
Treatment takes about two hours.

What happens in the treatment?
66 mL of the patient's peripheral blood is collected and centrifuged. The resultant platelet rich plasma (PRP) is exposed to a laser for 3 minutes and then re-infused back into the patient intravenously.

May VSEL treatment be repeated?
VSEL treatment may be repeated every 3 months.

Is the patient awake during the procedure?
The patient is fully awake and aware during the entire treament.

Does the treatment have side effects?
We know of no serious side effects from this treatment.

What is the cost of treatment?
The treatment costs $6,000. If a patient can not afford treatment, and their medical situation is critical, then we may provide treatment for less or for free.

How does your treatment compare to other clinics?
Most clinics in the United States charge $7,000 to $11,000 for the same treatment.

Will my insurance cover the cost of treatment?
Insurance companies do not cover stem cell therapy.
