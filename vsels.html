VSEL Stem Cells

Several Nobel Prizes have been awarded for the original and subsequent discoveries related to stem cells. This is perhaps medicine’s most promising area for anti-aging and life extension, along with possible human treatments. That said, this area is full of controversy and confusion due to the proliferation of products and claims.

An entire hierarchy of stem cells and their chemical products for regeneration needs to be understood to match properly a type of preparation for an intended clinical use. Claims can be made that suggest a person is getting stem cells that will integrate in their tissues as new youthful cells, and depending on the product and preparation there may be no living cells, nor cells that are either youthful or integrate in tissue. With a full understanding of what is offered for what purpose, an optimal match between the product or procedure and the intended use can be made.

Regeneration medicine protocols can be broadly divided between cellular and growth factor protocols. A cellular procedure provides living cells that in some cases may incorporate in tissue, or may primarily be a source of growth and regeneratiaon chemical factors that effect the intended results.

There’s a hierarchy of types and functions of stem cells that is complex and multifactorial that contributes to confusion in this area if not understood. The most powerful of all the stem cells are true embryonic stem cells. These are from the early stage forming embryo and are so dynamic they are called totipotent. This means that a single cell can create an entire new person. That’s an incredible amount of power and can be too powerful. This is the only type of stem cell prone to form benign tumors called teratomas. These can be giant hair balls in the abdomen or elsewhere, as big as a cantaloupe, with hair and skin and teeth and nails and any other body tissue rolled into a mass. This is the main reason that the use of these cells is avoided, until such time this tendency can be securely prevented.

The next most powerful type of stem cell, that is by contrast also safe to use, is called pluripotent. Pluri means many and a pluripotent cell is capable of becoming any type of cell in the body.

In the early embryo the cells segment into 3 distinct germ layers - ectoderm, endoderm, and mesoderm - each of which gives rise to particular cells and tissues in the ultimate newly formed body. Pluripotent stem cells can become any of the types of cells of any of the 3 germ layers, making them very broadly useful for regeneration medicine.

A pluripotent stem cell with extraordinary features is the so called VSEL, which stands for "very small embryonic-like" stem cell. The very important key is that these cells are embryonic-like, which means quite powerful, yet they are just past the embryonic stage and have been shown to be not prone to teratoma formation. This makes them the most potent cell that can thus far be used for general regeneration medicine. Depending upon the conditions in which they are cultured they have been conclusively proven to be able to generate cells of all 3 germ layers.

What is even more remarkable is that at or around the time we are born these cells go into a state of hibernation. They become dormant and are not dividing, which also means they are effectively not aging. Telomeres, the ends of the chromosomes that get smaller with cell replication, the core DNA aging clock, remain very long like those of newborn or very young cells. Whether someone is 5 or 50 or 100 years old these cells retain the energy and function of newborn caliber cells, as if the person had saved their umbilical cord blood throughout life.
